<p><p>Pulp and paper enzymes are essential in the production of paper and other related products. The market for these enzymes can be classified into different types based on their functionality. Amylase enzymes are used to break down starch, while cellulase enzymes assist in the breakdown of cellulose fibers. Xylanase enzymes target the degradation of xylan, a complex polysaccharide in plant cell walls. Lipase enzymes act on lipids, enhancing the removal of unwanted substances from the pulp. Additionally, there are other enzymes involved in various processes, such as lignin modification and color bleach, further contributing to the overall pulp and paper enzymes market.</p></p>
