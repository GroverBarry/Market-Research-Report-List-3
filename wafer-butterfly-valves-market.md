<p><p>Wafer butterfly valves are a type of quarter-turn valve that is commonly used in various industrial applications. These valves consist of a disk-shaped closing mechanism, or disc, that is positioned in the center of the pipe and can rotate to either block or allow the flow of fluids. The disc is connected to a stem that is operated manually or automatically through an actuator.</p><p>These valves are known for their versatility, compact design, and cost-effectiveness. They are widely used in industries such as oil and gas, water treatment, power generation, chemical processing, and HVAC systems. The wafer design allows for easy installation between flanges without the need for additional hardware.</p><p>The global market for wafer butterfly valves is anticipated to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><ul><li>Flanged Ends</li><li>Threaded Ends</li><li>Socket and Butt Welded Ends</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The wafer butterfly valves market is categorized into different types based on their end connections. Flanged ends are valves that have flanges on each end, allowing them to be bolted to pipes or equipment. Threaded ends have threads on each end, allowing them to be screwed into pipes or fittings. Socket ends have sockets on each end, allowing them to be connected to pipes or fittings by solvent welding. Butt welded ends are valves that have ends prepared for welding directly to pipes or fittings.</p></p>
