<p><p>The market for transesophageal ultrasound imagers includes various types of imaging technologies. The 2D imaging provides a two-dimensional view of the heart, allowing for inspection of its structure and function. 3D and 4D imaging offer enhanced visualization by creating three-dimensional images in real-time, enabling better assessment of heart chambers and valves. Doppler imaging measures blood flow and pressure within the heart, aiding in the detection of abnormalities. Other types may include color flow imaging, strain imaging, and contrast-enhanced imaging, which further assist in diagnosing heart conditions.</p></p>
