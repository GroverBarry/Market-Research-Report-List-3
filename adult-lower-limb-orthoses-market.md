<p><p>The adult lower limb orthoses market comprises two types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are devices that provide flexibility and support to the lower limbs, allowing for a range of motion and muscle movement. They are primarily used in cases involving muscle weakness or neurological disorders. On the other hand, static orthotics are rigid devices that provide stability and immobilization to the lower limbs. They are commonly prescribed for conditions such as fractures, sprains, and deformities. Both types play a crucial role in aiding rehabilitation and maximizing functional abilities in adults.</p></p>
