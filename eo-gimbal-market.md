<p><p>The EO gimbal market consists of two main types: 2-axis and 3-axis gimbals. Both types are designed to stabilize and control the movement of electro-optical (EO) devices such as cameras or sensors. A 2-axis gimbal offers stabilization along two axes, typically pitch and roll, ensuring steady footage or image capture. On the other hand, a 3-axis gimbal provides stabilization along three axes - pitch, roll, and yaw - offering even greater control over camera movement and eliminating unwanted jitter or shake. These gimbals find applications in various industries such as photography, videography, surveillance, and aerial imaging.</p></p>
