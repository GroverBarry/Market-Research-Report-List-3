<p><p>Polyvinyl Butyral (PVB) is a polymer material mainly used in making laminated safety glass, such as car windshields. Its production involves two types of processes: alkaline alcoholysis and acidic alcoholysis. In alkaline alcoholysis, PVB is treated with an alkaline solution, resulting in a high viscosity resin used in laminated glasses. Acidic alcoholysis involves treating PVB with an acid, resulting in low viscosity resin used in coatings and adhesives. Both processes help create versatile PVB products catered to different applications in the construction and automotive industry.</p></p>
