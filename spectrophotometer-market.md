<p><p>Spectrophotometers are used to measure the amount of light absorbed or transmitted by a sample, helping to determine its chemical composition. In the market, two popular types are UV-Visible Spectrophotometers and IR Spectrophotometers. UV-Visible Spectrophotometers analyze the visible and ultraviolet regions of the electromagnetic spectrum, allowing for the analysis of a wide range of compounds. On the other hand, IR Spectrophotometers analyze the infrared region, providing valuable information about molecular structures and functional groups. Both types are widely used in various industries, such as pharmaceuticals, environmental testing, and materials science.</p></p>
