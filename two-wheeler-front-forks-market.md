<p><p>The two-wheeler front forks market offers various types of forks, including fixed and telescopic forks. Fixed forks are rigid and do not have any mechanism to absorb shocks or vibrations. They are commonly found on older or low-budget motorcycles. In contrast, telescopic forks consist of two tubes, with the outer one sliding into the inner one. This design allows for compression and extension, providing better suspension and shock absorption. Telescopic forks are more commonly used in modern motorcycles due to their improved ride quality and handling capabilities.</p></p>
