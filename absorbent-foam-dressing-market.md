<p><p>Absorbent foam dressing is a type of wound dressing that is designed to effectively manage exudate (fluid) from wounds. It is primarily used in the treatment of moderate to heavily exuding wounds, such as pressure ulcers, venous leg ulcers, and surgical wounds. These dressings are usually made of soft, non-adherent foam materials that absorb and retain large amounts of fluid while maintaining a moist wound environment.</p><p>The absorbent foam dressing market has been witnessing steady growth in recent years. <p><p>Absorbent foam dressing is categorized into two types: without adhesive border and with adhesive border. In the case of without adhesive border, the foam dressing does not have a sticky border and is secured with other means, such as tape or a bandage. On the other hand, the with adhesive border variant has a sticky border that adheres to the surrounding skin, providing a secure and convenient dressing option. Both types serve the purpose of absorbing exudate from wounds, promoting healing and maintaining a moist environment.</p></p>
