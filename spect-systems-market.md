<p><p>SPECT (Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography) Systems are medical imaging devices used to diagnose and monitor various conditions. The market for SPECT Systems can be categorized into two types: Hybrid SPECT and Standalone SPECT. </p><p>Hybrid SPECT refers to a system that combines SPECT technology with other imaging techniques such as CT (Computed Tomography) or MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging). This integration allows for improved image quality and more accurate diagnoses.</p><p>On the other hand, Standalone SPECT refers to systems that solely utilize SPECT technology without any integration with other imaging techniques. These systems are typically more affordable and simpler in design, making them suitable for smaller healthcare facilities or clinics with limited resources.</p><p>Both Hybrid SPECT and Standalone SPECT systems play crucial roles in the medical imaging market by providing valuable diagnostic information, albeit with different capabilities and cost considerations.</p></p>
