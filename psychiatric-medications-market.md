<p><p>Psychiatric medications can be classified into four main types. Antidepressants are medications used to alleviate symptoms of depression by increasing the levels of certain chemicals in the brain. Anti-anxiety medications are prescribed to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation by slowing down the central nervous system. Mood-stabilizing medications help manage mood swings and treat bipolar disorder by regulating neurotransmitters in the brain. Antipsychotic medications are used to treat conditions like schizophrenia, helping to control hallucinations, delusions, and other psychotic symptoms by blocking certain neurotransmitters.</p></p>
