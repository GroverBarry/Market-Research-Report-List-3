<p><p>Prostate-specific Antigen (PSA) Testing is primarily done through two methods - CLIA (Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments) and ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay). The CLIA method involves testing blood samples in a clinical laboratory using advanced equipment, ensuring high accuracy and reliability of results. On the other hand, the ELISA method uses a biochemical assay technique to detect and measure PSA levels in blood samples. Both methods play a crucial role in diagnosing prostate cancer, aiding in early detection and subsequent treatment.</p></p>
