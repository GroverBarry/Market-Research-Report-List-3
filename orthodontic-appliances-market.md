<p><p>Orthodontic appliances refer to devices used by orthodontists to treat misaligned teeth and jaws. There are two main types: removable appliances and fixed appliances. Removable orthodontic appliances, as the name suggests, can be taken out by the patient. They are commonly used for mild dental conditions or as a preliminary treatment before fixed appliances. In contrast, fixed orthodontic appliances are attached to the teeth and cannot be removed by the patient. They are typically used for more complex dental cases and provide continuous force to gradually align the teeth and jaws.</p></p>
