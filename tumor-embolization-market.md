<p><p>Tumor embolization is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat certain types of cancer. It involves blocking or reducing the blood supply to a tumor, causing it to shrink or die. This is done by inserting tiny particles or a gel-like substance into the blood vessels that supply the tumor, preventing the tumor from receiving the necessary nutrients and oxygen.</p><p>The tumor embolization market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to various factors such as the increasing prevalence of cancer, advancements in technology, rising geriatric population, and the growing demand for minimally invasive procedures. Additionally, the rising adoption of tumor embolization as an alternative to surgical procedures and the development of new embolic agents are further expected to drive market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing several latest trends and developments. One such trend is the increasing use of advanced imaging techniques such as cone-beam CT (CBCT) and 3D rotational angiography to improve the accuracy and outcomes of tumor embolization procedures. These imaging techniques provide real-time visualization of blood vessels and tumor structures, aiding in better decision-making and precise embolization.</p><p>Another notable trend is the introduction of new embolic agents with improved characteristics. For instance, drug-eluting beads (DEBs) are gaining prominence due to their ability to deliver chemotherapeutic drugs directly to the tumor, resulting in enhanced tumor shrinkage and improved patient outcomes.</p><p>Overall, the tumor embolization market is projected to witness substantial growth in the coming years, driven by factors such as increasing cancer prevalence, advancements in technology, and the adoption of minimally invasive procedures.
