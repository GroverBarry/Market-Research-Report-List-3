<p><p>A molecular beacon probe is a type of fluorescent probe used in molecular biology and genetics to detect specific nucleic acid sequences in a sample. It consists of a single-stranded DNA or RNA molecule that is designed to bind to a target DNA or RNA sequence of interest. The molecule is structured in such a way that it forms a stem-loop structure, keeping the fluorescent dye and the quencher molecule close together, resulting in minimal fluorescence. When the molecular beacon probe binds to its target sequence, it undergoes a conformational change, causing the stem-loop to open up and separate the dye and quencher molecules, leading to fluorescence.</p><p>The global molecular beacon probe market is witnessing steady growth due to the increasing application of these probes in diagnostic and research laboratories. <p><ul><li>Tag Molecular Beacon</li><li>Label-free Molecular Beacon</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Molecular beacon probe market consists of two main types: Tag Molecular Beacon and Label-free Molecular Beacon. Tag Molecular Beacon utilizes a fluorophore to emit a fluorescent signal when bound to its target DNA or RNA sequence. This allows for easy detection and visualization of the target. On the other hand, Label-free Molecular Beacon does not use a fluorophore. Instead, it relies on a conformational change in its structure upon target binding, resulting in a detectable signal. Both types play crucial roles in molecular diagnostics and research applications.</p></p>
