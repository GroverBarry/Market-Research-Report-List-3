<p><p>The thin-film power inductor market can be divided into two types: Surface Mount Technology (SMT) and Through Hole Technology. SMT refers to a method of attaching electronic components to a printed circuit board (PCB) without drilling holes, which improves efficiency and reduces size. SMT thin-film power inductors are smaller and lighter, making them suitable for compact devices. Through Hole Technology, on the other hand, involves mounting components by inserting their leads into drilled holes in the PCB. Through Hole thin-film power inductors are generally larger and are commonly used in applications that require high power and reliability.</p></p>
