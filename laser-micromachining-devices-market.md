<p><p>Laser micromachining devices are high-precision tools used to perform small-scale material processing in various industries. These devices are categorized into different types based on the laser's pulse duration: nanosecond, picosecond, and femtosecond. </p><p>Nanosecond lasers emit pulses lasting billionths of a second, making them suitable for tasks like metal marking and engraving. Picosecond lasers have shorter pulses in trillionths of a second, enabling precise cutting and drilling of materials like glass and ceramics. Femtosecond lasers, with pulses lasting quadrillionths of a second, are utilized for delicate operations like eye surgery and microelectronics fabrication.</p></p>
