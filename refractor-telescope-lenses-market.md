<p><ul><li>Concave</li><li>Convex</li><li>Concave-convex</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Refractor Telescope Lenses market consists of three types of lenses: concave, convex, and concave-convex. Concave lenses are thinner at the center and thicker at the edges, causing light rays to diverge. Convex lenses, on the other hand, are thicker at the center and thinner at the edges, resulting in light rays converging. Concave-convex lenses combine these two shapes, allowing for more precise control of light refraction. Each lens type offers specific advantages in telescopes, depending on the desired magnification and image quality.</p></p>
